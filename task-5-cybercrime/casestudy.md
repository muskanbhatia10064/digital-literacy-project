# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest growing 
cybercrimes in India, targeting college students, working professionals, 
and senior citizens alike.

## How It Typically Happens

1. The fraudster contacts the victim via phone, WhatsApp, or SMS
   posing as a bank representative, online seller, or scholarship officer.
2. They create a sense of urgency — claiming the victim's account 
   will be blocked, or that a scholarship/refund needs to be processed.
3. The victim is asked to share their UPI PIN, OTP, or to scan a 
   QR code sent by the fraudster.
4. Once the victim scans the QR code or shares the OTP, money is 
   instantly debited from their account.
5. The fraudster disappears immediately after the transaction.

## Who Is Usually Targeted
First-time UPI users, college students receiving scholarships, 
online shoppers, and people selling items on platforms like OLX 
or Facebook Marketplace.

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss with little chance of recovery
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital payments
- In severe cases, victims lose entire savings
- Legal complications if the fraud involves identity misuse

## Source
Based on cases reported by the National Cyber Crime Portal 
(cybercrime.gov.in) and RBI consumer awareness guidelines.
